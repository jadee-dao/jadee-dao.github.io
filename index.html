About Me

I am a first-year PhD student in Computer Science at Stanford SAIL! Currently, I am rotating with Prof. Marco Pavone at the Autonomous Systems Lab, where I study how reasoning techniques can improve decision-making and perception in robotics. I am broadly interested in making robots and agents more reliable and generalizable in dynamic, complex, unseen environments.

Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies at Caltech, where I focused on sensor algorithms for wearable systems and multimodal learning. I have also spent time as a research intern at Google, Apple, and NASA JPL.

I am fortunate to be supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship and a Stanford Graduate Fellowship. I also am a venture capital fellow with Pear VC.
